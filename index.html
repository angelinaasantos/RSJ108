How to interview for a story...

Preparation Time: 30 minutes to 2 hours
Helpfullness rating: 100%

First time interviewing someone for a story? Don’t worry! Here are 7 steps and an a video with additional tips for journalistic interviewing.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Device: Laptop, Tablet/iPad, or Phone
  • Search Engine to Research
  • Paper & Pen to Take Notes

  1. Research what is your story topic and/or person

    Before you can write out good questions to ask someone who you are interviewing for an article, podcast, or another medium, you need to gather all the information you can. This is the time to write down your objective for the interview.

  2. Decide who you want to interview

    Search for your interviewee online and see if there are other articles about this person. Write down a bio about your interviewee. Highlight specific milestones that you want to talk about.

  3. Find their contact information

    In order to schedule an interview, you need to have a point of contact. The best way to approach this is to look for their email.

  4. Create questions on the topic and information you want to cover for the story

    When writing out your questions, you want to create ones that allow the conversation or interview to flow naturally.

    Create questions that hit all 5 W's.

  5. Draft and write an email to the person or company

    Email Template:

    Hello, [Insert Interviewee Name]! I’m [Your Name], and I am a student here at the University of Nevada, Reno. I am currently [describe the purpose of your task].I would like to write about yourself and the role you have in [business, non-profit, etc.] to write about advocacy and raising awareness for [the purpose of the story].

    Would you available for an in-person interview (around 10 mins or less) to talk about [topic] by the [date] of next week? I’m interested to learn more about the foundation and the outreach they do for the community. I believe that your position on the board will give valuable insight on [topic].

    Hope to hear from you soon.

    [Your Name]

    [Any Additional Contact Information]

  6. Schedule a time to conduct the interview. In-person or over call/zoom

    Ensure it is a time that works for both yourself and the interviewee. Something to keep in mind is to keep yourself free for at least an hour after the scheduled time. This is so there is no feel to rush through the interview.

  7. Record and conduct the interview

    It is necessary to make sure your interviewee is okay with you recording the interview.

  8. Check out this Youtube for 30 Tips on Interviewing!

    Hear from Sparks Media Solutions on advice and techniques to practice when getting ready to interview someone.
